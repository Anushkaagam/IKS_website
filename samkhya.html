Deep Dive: Samkhya Philosophy

Since ancient times, Indians have been searching for answers to life’s mysteries. The Samkhya philosophy, attributed to Sage Kapila, is one of the oldest and most analytical systems. It emphasizes numbers and empirical reality. The word 'Samkhya' means 'number' or 'enumeration', referring to its method of categorizing the elements of existence.

The Philosophy of Samkhya

Kapila Muni is considered the founder of Samkhya and is described in sacred texts like the Bhagavad Gita as a sage with deep yogic insight.

The philosophy identifies dual realities—Purusha (consciousness) and Prakriti (matter)—and proposes the evolution of the universe through their interaction. It recognizes subtle and physical bodies and introduces the concept of reincarnation through subtle body transfer.

There are four parts to the subtle body: Buddhi (consciousness), Ahankara (ego), Manas (mind), and Prana (breath). This is illustrated in detailed models explaining the flow of creation.

Each phase of Samkhya development stems from the attraction of Purusha towards Prakriti. It explains the emergence of the universe and the three gunas—Tamas (inertia), Rajas (activity), and Sattva (purity).

Conclusion: Among the six darshanas of Hinduism, Samkhya is the oldest. Referenced in Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Bhagavad Gita, its logical approach has preserved it through debates and time. It remains a cornerstone of Indian philosophical thought.
